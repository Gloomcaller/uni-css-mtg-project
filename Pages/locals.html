Metagame & Matchups

Understanding the local metagame and knowing how to handle various matchups is crucial for success. Here are a few tips and strategies against some of the popular decks you might face in your local scene.

  • Adapt your sideboard depending on the most popular decks at your locals.
  • Make sure your deck is well-rounded with enough removal and card advantage tools to suit various decks at yout local turnament store or club.
  • Some cards like Bojuka Bog and Relic of Progenitus are useless if there isn't a single Storm or other graveyard heavy deck at your locals.
  • It's always wise to get to know your local patrons so you can know what they play and their habits and strategies, and don't just be a spy make some acquaintances while you are having fun !

These are just some of my suggestions on how I think you should sideboard versus some decks in the metagame.

Kuldotha Rebirth

The Kuldotha Rebirth has evolved over time gaining considerable power with a combination of strong goblins and artifacts like Experimental Synthesizer so it's no mystery why it's seeing a 16.6% play in turnaments.

While we do have a decent life gain with Vampire Sovereign and Spinning Darkness Fangren Marauder may seem expensive, but if you can get it down, it will most likely win you the game. Gaining effectively two burn spells worth of life by sacrificing your treasure token or wellspring is incredibly powerful. Not to mention every time they want to sacrifice their Synthesizer or implement it comes at the cost of giving you 5 life.

Affinity

Basic game plan against Affinity is to try to answer all their important creatures and time it right with Bojuka Bogs and Relic of Progenitus, so they don't get the opportunity to recur with Blood Fountain.

Maximizing on Avenging Hunters is key, because the Undercity is great at stealing games from under their feet. Hopefully you can get Avenging Hunter down before they flood the board with free 4/4 creatures. Marauder and Deglamer are great cards here just for disrupting their game plan wherever possible.

Caw Gates

This match up should be fairly favoured. They have surprisingly few threats and very little permission, so answering every threat can be relatively easy. However, throwing away Chainer's Edict can be a sure way to lose this match up. You can just lose to a surprise Guardian of the Guildpact.

Terror

Fairly straight forward sideboarding here. Relic of Progenitus is great at slowing down the Terror Gameplan. Duress is great at making sure you get to take the troublesome spells like Counterspell and Chainer's Edict.

All those are basically free as Spinning Darkness and Fungal Infection doesn't answer anything so you can side them out.

Faeries

Drown in Sorrow is obviously a great card for dealing with the faeries in the air, which should in turn allow the Chainer's Edict and other removal spells to deal with the Ninjas.

Avenging Hunter is a great card here for finishing the game and killing your opponent even through Snaps. However, with all their combat tricks and flash creatures, you really need to make sure you can answer everything they could before playing it.
